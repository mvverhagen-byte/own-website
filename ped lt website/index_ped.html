Post-Transplant DUS Reference Values

Timepoint-specific Doppler-ultrasound reference values (5th–95th percentile) after pediatric liver transplantation, stratified by graft type and age. Based on a consecutive cohort of 112 patients (123 LTs), UMCG 2015–2020.

Hepatic Vein VPI — Trend Over Time

Median VPI from Day 0 to Year 2 (all grafts). Note the characteristic dip at Days 4–6.

Alarm Thresholds — Warrants Further Imaging or Surgical Exploration

HA — Stenosis suspected Post-anastomotic PSV > 200 cm/s AND tardus parvus waveform (RI < 0.5, systolic acceleration time > 80 ms)
PV — Stenosis suspected Anastomotic PSV > 125 cm/s, OR pre-to-anastomotic velocity ratio ≥ 4
HV — Outflow obstruction Monophasic waveform: VPI = 0
Any vessel — Thrombosis Absent flow in any vessel always warrants immediate further imaging and surgical consultation

Clinical Notes — Graft & Age Effects

Healthy Children — Native Liver Reference Values

Hepatic hilum DUS reference values in 100 healthy children aged 0–17 years (no liver or cardiac pathology). Measurements at hepatic hilum after 4h fast. HA PSV and RI are age-dependent; PV PSV is not.

ℹ
Key finding: Portal vein PSV is not age-dependent (overall 5th–95th: 14–41 cm/s). Hepatic artery PSV and RI both decrease significantly with age. Use age-group specific values for HA measurements.
Age Group PV PSV (cm/s) — Median (5th–95th) HA PSV (cm/s) — Median (5th–95th) HA RI — Median (5th–95th)
All ages (0–17 y) 26 (14 – 41) 55 (23 – 116) 0.72 (0.57 – 0.86)
≤ 2 years 27 (15 – 41) 60 (30 – 123) 0.74 (0.55 – 0.89)
2 – 12 years 26 (12 – 39) 58 (22 – 90) 0.73 (0.64 – 0.86)
> 12 years 26 (15 – 49) 46 (25 – 108) 0.70 (0.55 – 0.81)

Notes

  • All measurements at hepatic hilum, Doppler angle < 60°, fasted 4h
  • PV PSV shows no significant age-dependence (β = −0.056, p = 0.68) across 0–17 years
  • HA PSV decreases significantly with age (β = −0.873 cm/s/yr, p = 0.04)
  • HA RI decreases significantly with age (β = −0.004/yr, p < 0.001)
  • HA RI in >12y (0.55–0.81) mirrors the adult reference range reported in fasted adults
  • Values outside 5th–95th percentile warrant clinical correlation — may still be physiological

Adult Context — For Comparison

Adult RI range (published) 0.55 – 0.81 (fasted healthy adults). Comparable to >12 years group in Verhagen 2023.
Adult PV PSV range (published) Mean ~21 cm/s (SD 8), or 16–40 cm/s. Our pediatric 5th–95th (14–41) aligns well.

Key Methodological Notes

  • HA: post-anastomotic measurement of donor artery proximal to liver hilum (anastomosis often not visible)
  • PV: measured at anastomosis (or narrowest extrahepatic caliber if not visible)
  • HV: measured 2 cm proximal to anastomosis; VPI = (A−B)/A per Chong et al.
  • VPI 0 = monophasic; VPI >0 to <1 = biphasic; VPI ≥ 1 = triphasic (normal target)
  • All DUS performed on Canon Aplio 500, Doppler angle < 60°
  • Post-first-week examinations: patients fasted prior to DUS
  • Reference values include only patients with no complications during 2-year follow-up
